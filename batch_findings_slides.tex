\vspace{0.35em}
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.72\textwidth}
\scriptsize
\textbf{Comments.}
The pooled $C=1$ model mixes PA and FE reference points and gives a low
$\lambda=0.538$. Moving to $C=2$ reveals a sharp split: a smaller FE-dominated,
low-$\lambda$ group and a larger PA-dominated, high-$\lambda$ group. The $C=3$
run further divides the population into one LE/high-$\lambda$ cluster and two
more FE-oriented moderate-$\lambda$ clusters, but those labels should be read
cautiously because $C=3$ is not label-sorted in the code.
\end{minipage}
\hfill
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.25\textwidth}
\scriptsize
\textbf{Fit/diagnostics.}\\
Log ML: $C=1$ -99,810; $C=2$ -99,114; $C=3$ -98,848.\\
$C=1$ passes diagnostics; $C=2/3$ improve fit but have weak SMC diagnostics
($\hat R_{\max}=1.56/2.46$, min ESS/chain $=1.7/1.2$).
\end{minipage}

\vspace{0.35em}
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.72\textwidth}
\scriptsize
\textbf{Comments.}
Without the California-mix lotteries, the pooled model shifts toward higher
$\lambda$ and a diffuse reference point. The $C=2$ structure is especially
clear: one FE-dominated, low-$\lambda$ group and one LE-dominated, high-$\lambda$
group. In $C=3$, the FE group remains distinct, while the higher-$\lambda$
mass is split into two LE-leaning clusters, one with moderate $\lambda=1.139$
and one with the strongest loss aversion, $\lambda=1.454$.
\end{minipage}

$C=1$ passes diagnostics; $C=2/3$ improve fit but have weak SMC diagnostics
($\hat R_{\max}=2.08/2.28$, min ESS/chain $=1.3/1.2$).
\end{minipage}
